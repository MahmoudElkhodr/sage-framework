About the Conference

SAGE 2026 marks the inaugural international symposium dedicated to structured approaches for integrating generative AI in higher education. Building on the empirically-validated SAGE (Structured AI-Guided Education) framework, this conference brings together researchers, educators, instructional designers, and technology leaders to share evidence-based practices, innovative implementations, and emerging research in AI-enhanced pedagogy.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions on all aspects of generative AI in education, including but not limited to the following themes:

Theme 1: Pedagogical Frameworks & Models

  • AI orchestration competencies and skill development
  • Framework design and validation studies
  • Constructivist and situated learning approaches
  • Bloom's taxonomy applications in AI contexts

Theme 2: Assessment & Academic Integrity

  • AI-integrated assessment design
  • Authentic assessment strategies
  • Detection vs. integration approaches
  • Evaluation rubrics for AI-enhanced work

Theme 3: Disciplinary Applications

  • SAGE framework adaptations across fields
  • Discipline-specific implementation case studies
  • AI literacy in professional education
  • Technical, health, business, and humanities contexts

Theme 4: Student Learning & Outcomes

  • AI literacy development
  • Critical thinking with AI tools
  • Student perceptions and experiences
  • Equity and accessibility considerations

Theme 5: Institutional Policy & Practice

  • University-wide AI integration strategies
  • Policy development and governance
  • Faculty development and support
  • Ethical frameworks and guidelines

Theme 6: Emerging Technologies & Future Directions

  • Multimodal AI in education (text, image, video, code)
  • AI agents and educational applications
  • Learning analytics and AI
  • Future research agendas

Submission Guidelines

Submission Types:

  • Full Research Papers: 5-8 pages (presenting complete research with methodology, results, and conclusions)
  • Short Papers & Work-in-Progress: 5-6 pages (preliminary findings, ongoing research, or emerging ideas)
  • Case Studies & Implementation Reports: 5-8 pages (detailed accounts of practical implementations)

Format Requirements:

  • Format: IEEE Conference format (download templates)
  • Page Limits: Minimum 5 pages, maximum 8 pages
  • Review Process: Single-blind peer review (minimum 2 reviewers per paper)
  • Language: English
  • Submission Platform: EasyChair (link to be provided)
📝 Submission Platform: All papers must be submitted through EasyChair. The submission link will be activated in early May 2026.
