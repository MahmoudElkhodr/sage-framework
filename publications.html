Evidence & Impact

The empirical case for SAGE

SAGE is an empirically formed assessment-design framework grounded in visible student judgement — this page presents the empirical basis, sector alignment, publications and public commentary supporting the program.

Evidence base

The empirical foundation

SAGE was not designed from theory alone — it was shaped iteratively through classroom redesign, student data collection, cross-campus evaluation, and reflective refinement across three countries and multiple cohorts.

How SAGE was formed
1
Classroom redesign
Assessment tasks were redesigned to embed AI explicitly, requiring students to document, verify, and defend their AI-assisted work as part of the assessment process.
2
Student evidence collection
Empirical data was gathered on how students actually engaged with AI — including verification behaviours, revision patterns, dependency levels, and how they exercised judgement.
3
Evaluation
Findings were evaluated against learning outcomes, academic integrity criteria, and sector expectations, across institutions in Australia, China, and the United Kingdom.
4
Refinement
SAGE evolved through successive cycles, with each iteration informing the pedagogical structure, policy language, and student-facing resources that constitute the current framework.
